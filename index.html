About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at the University of Notre Dame, specializing in hardware systems, PCB design, embedded systems, and biomedical imaging. I develop compact, wearable devices for real-time tissue imaging using FD-NIRS technology and CMOS ASIC integration.

Projects

  • Wearable FD-NIRS Device: Designed a compact dual-wavelength optical imaging system using custom CMOS ASICs, RF/DC switching, and embedded firmware.
  • High-Density Brain Imaging Array: Developed modular source-detector architecture for scalable optical brain monitoring systems.
